Glossary


Dataset:

A data table for some indicator over a time period, where each row is a time series and each column is a time point, e.g. year, and the cells contain the value of the indicator for a particular individual (entity, being or object) and time point.


Indicator:

A quantitative characteristic of individual. Indicators can vary over time. Called "variables" in statistics. Examples: the population, unemployment rate or average life expectancy of a country.


Metric:

Is a complex indicator defined as mathematical function (formula) using indicators or other metrics. Typically the weighted sum of normalized indicators values. For example, health might be defined as

health = 0.6 * nle(lifeExpectancy) + 0.4 * ncm(childMortality)

where nle and ncm are functions normalizing the life expectancy and child mortality to real numbers in the range [0.0,1.0].

Applying this to a specific dataset for a country x at time t would be defined as

health(x,t) = 0.6 * nle(lifeExpectancy(x,t)) + 0.4 * ncm(childMortality(x,t))


Metadata of:

Description of performed according a predefined structure.


Individual:

A term denoting a particular entity, being or object. A proper noun. Examples: London, Germany, European Union, Angela Merkel.


Causal Model:

An abstract model describing the causal relations between concepts.


Event:

A historical event potentially influenced the values of indicators. For example, court decision, entry into force of a law, natural disaster, etc.


Visualisation:

Visual representation of datasets in form of tables, charts, etc.
